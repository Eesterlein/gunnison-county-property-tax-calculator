Important Information

Taxes cannot be appealed.

The tax amount is calculated using:

  • The property's actual value, determined by the Assessor's Office
  • Assessment rates, which are set by Colorado state law
  • Mill levies, which are set by local taxing authorities

The Gunnison County Assessor's Office does not set assessment rates or mill levies.

Actual value can be appealed. Appeals relate only to actual (market) value. If you believe your property's actual value is incorrect, you may appeal the assessment. This applies to all property types (residential, commercial, vacant land).

Appeal Period

Appeals must be filed May 1 through June 8. Late appeals cannot be accepted.

How to Submit an Appeal

Appeals must be submitted in writing. You may submit your appeal:

  • In person at the Gunnison County Assessor's Office
  • By email to assessor@gunnisoncounty.org
  • By mail to the Gunnison County Assessor's Office

Supporting Evidence

Before filing an appeal, review your property record in the Property Record Search and your Notice of Valuation to confirm the information on file is correct.

You may compare your property to the reappraisal sales used during the valuation process to evaluate whether your value aligns with recent market activity.

Appeals must be submitted in writing by June 8.

Official Appeal Information – Gunnison County

Exemptions and Adjustments

Exemptions (such as senior exemptions) and adjustments (such as special district adjustments) are separate from the assessment appeal process. If you have questions about exemptions or adjustments, contact the Gunnison County Assessor's Office.

Mill Levies & Taxing Authorities

Mill levies are set by taxing authorities, not the Assessor. The Assessor's Office determines property values, but taxing authorities (county, municipalities, school districts, special districts) set mill levies that determine the tax rate.

If you believe the actual value is correct but have concerns about mill levies, you should contact the applicable taxing authorities.
